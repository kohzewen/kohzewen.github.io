---
title: "Assessing the Detectability of Europa’s Seafloor Topography from Europa Clipper’s Gravity Data (2022)"
date: 2022-08-19
venue: "Planetary Science Journal"
authors: "<strong>Z. Koh</strong>, F. Nimmo, J.I. Lunine, E. Mazarico, A.J. Dombard"
pdf: "/files/2022-europa.pdf"
paperurl: "https://iopscience.iop.org/article/10.3847/PSJ/ac82aa"
excerpt: "Due to its size and tidal heating, Europa may possess active volcanism and hydrothermal circulation at its seafloor — signs of geologic activity and potential habitability. We create a suite of models to assess the ability of Europa Clipper's gravity measurements to determine Europa's seafloor topography. We find that seafloor topography should be detectable at Clipper's resolution, while ice shell characteristics are not resolvable globally."
